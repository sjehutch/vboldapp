Glossary



Area: A section within a structure or the whole building. This could be a floor, a room or section of the structure. Each Area can have a separate temperature, estimated HLT, and volume. This is a required entry field for calculation of dosage.

Calculate: The button on the Fumigation Dosage Plan tab that computes and displays the total amount of fumigant, average concentration and average CT needed in the Results box. Also pressing the Calculate button will compute and display the Fumigant required, Target CT and Initial Target Concentration by area in the Structure/Area Info box. Any box on the Fumigation Dosage Plan tab preceded with an "*" are required input fields before any calculation can be performed.

Contact: A person employed at the Customer or Location.

Customer Information: The first tab in the ProFume Fumiguide where address, contact details and notes can be stored for the fumigation.

Commodity: The food inside the fumigated space.

CT Achieved: The dosage accumulated from Start of Exposure to the last concentration reading. The CT Achieved is compared to the Target CT and Projected CT for Status Recommendations in the Monitoring Status tab.

Cylinder Temp: is the temperature of cylinders. This temperature can be estimated by the air temperature if cylinders are not in direct sunlight and have had time to equilibrate to ambient temperature.

Cylinder Pressure: is the pressure of the cylinder that can be read directly from a pressure gauge if placed as the part of the introduction line set up.

Diffusion: The spontaneous process whereby a fumigant moves from an area of high concentration toward an area of lower concentration.

Dosage: Refers to the number of oz-h/MCF or g-h/m3 accumulated during the exposure period. Dosage is interchangeable with CT. Types of dosage include: Target Dosage, Projected and Achieved Dosage.

Dose: The concentration/volume (ounces/MCF or g/ m3) amount of fumigant introduced into the fumigation space — oz/MCF (see Initial Target Concentration).

Elapsed Time: The period of time since the Start of Exposure to the last monitoring record entered for a monitoring point.

End Exposure is the time at which the accumulation of CT (dosage) ends.

End Time of HLT: Time of last concentration reading for each monitoring point.

Equilibrium: The state when all fumigant concentration readings are approximately equal across all fumigated areas.

Exposure, End: The time when target exposure was planned to be achieved. CT accumulation ends at this time. This event is user designated on the Monitoring Data Input and Monitoring Status tabs.

Exposure, Start: The time when CT accumulation begins. Usually planned to be the time equilibrium is expected. This event is user designated on the Monitoring Data Input and Monitoring Status tabs.

Exposure Time: The amount of time (exposure period) a fumigant is confined in a structure to kill the target pest. This is a required entry field for calculation of dosage.

Fan Capacity: The maximum air flow output of a fan.

Fog-Out: The condensation of moisture inside a fumigated structure that is caused by the air temperature reaching dew point. Methods to prevent a fog-out include: (1) using the proper inside diameter and length of the introduction hose, and (2) using appropriate fans with sufficient velocity to effectively mix the warmer air inside the structure with the cooler ProFume laden air.

Fumigant Required: The quantity of fumigant needed by area to reach Target CT per the fumigation conditions defined by the user.

Fumigation Type: Within the ProFume Fumiguide there are two fumigation types: commodity and space. These two options address the target of the fumigation either the building (space) or commodity within a fumigation chamber. This is a required entry field for calculation of dosage. Target Dosage for "commodity" is greater than for "space."

Fumiscope: A thermal conductivity analyzer used to monitor fumigant concentrations during the fumigation.

Fumigant Introduction: Release of the fumigant from its cylinders into the fumigation space.

Fumigation Dosage Plan: Stores all information and variables pertaining to the calculation of a fumigant dose and dosage. This is the second tab within the ProFume Fumiguide.

Graph: The eighth tab in the ProFume Fumiguide for graphing the concentration by time for each monitoring point.

Initial Target Concentration: The target dose [concentration/volume (ounces/MCF or g/ m3)] (concentration (ounces/MCF) that will achieve the Target CT based on the fumigation target and conditions input by the user.

Inside Diameter: The measurement of the interior diameter of the fumigant introduction and monitoring hoses.

Introduction History: The fifth tab in the ProFume Fumiguide for recording quantities of fumigant released in the various areas of a structure. . No inputs are required on this tab.

Introduction Line: The tubing by which fumigant is released from outside the structure or chamber into the fumigated area.

Introduction Plan: The third tab in the ProFume Fumiguide for entering introduction lines, fans and calculating actual and permitted introduction rates for various areas of the structure.

Introduction Rate: The rate (pounds per minute or kg/m) that the fumigant is planned to be released into the fumigated area.

Introduction, Start: The time at which introduction of fumigant into a Structure starts. This event is user designated on the Monitoring Data Input and Monitoring Status tabs.

Job Name: An optional user defined designation of a fumigation within the Fumigation Dosage Plan tab. Graph-The eighth tab in the ProFume Fumiguide for graphing the concentration by time for each monitoring point.

Half-Loss Time (HLT): Time required to lose one-half of the fumigant concentration, measured in hours.

Length: the linear measurement of introduction and monitoring lines in the Introduction Plan tab.

Licensed Fumigator: The person employed by a fumigation company, food company or group who has legal responsibility for conducting the fumigation.

Load Factor: An estimated percentage of the fumigated space filled by commodity.

MCF: 1,000 cubic feet.

Monitored Fumigation: Repeated observations of a fumigation during the exposure period to determine gas concentrations at specific locations, detect gas loss over time, ensure the Target CT is reached.

Monitoring Data: Concentration readings associated with specific dates and times by monitoring point recorded for a fumigation.

Monitoring Data Input: The sixth tab in the ProFume Fumiguide for entering monitoring data.

Monitoring Plan: The fourth tab within the ProFume Fumiguide where monitoring line location is set up (monitoring points per areas).

Monitoring Point: A location of a monitoring line where concentration readings are taken within the fumigated structure.

Monitoring Status: The seventh tab in the ProFume Fumiguide where elapsed time, HLT, Start & End time of HLT, CT achieved/projected and status/recommendations are displayed by monitoring point.

Next Step: The button on the lower right hand corner of all ProFume Fumiguide tabs, except the Monitoring Status tab, which will advance the fumigator to the next tab within the program.

Permitted Introduction Rate: The maximum rate of fumigant release preventing a fog-out condition in the fumigated space.

Post-Embryonic: Insect life stages after the egg stage (larvae, pupae, adult).

Precision Fumigation: optimizing fumigant use to maximize efficiency and minimize risk. Precision Fumigation is achieved by integrating all the factors affecting control, such as pest biology, temperature, exposure time, and improved sealing techniques into the fumigation management plan.

Pressure Type: Two fumigation pressure options are available in the ProFume Fumiguide-Normal Atmospheric or Vacuum. This is a required entry field for calculation of Target Dosage.

Projected CT: The forecasted CT expected to be accumulated within the planned exposure time based on actual HLT and concentration readings.

Relative Humidity (RH): The percentage of water present in the air relative to the amount it could hold at 100% saturation.

Start Exposure is the time at which the accumulation of CT (dosage) begins. Generally this should be at equilibrium.

Start Introduction is the time fumigant introduction into a structure or chamber begins/began.

Start Time of HLT: The most recent time at which the concentration peaked (reached a maximum concentration) for a monitoring point.

Status/Recommendations: Based on the dosage accumulated or CT Achieved at a point in time during the fumigation. The CT Achieved is compared to the Target CT and Projected CT for Status Recommendations in the Monitoring Status tab.

Structure: A freestanding building, storage facility, chamber, etc.

Sulfuryl Fluoride: The active ingredient of ProFume, which is non-flammable, non-corrosive and does not cause undesirable odors, is a gas at temperatures above -67°F.

Target CT: The number of oz-h/MCF or g-h/m3 targeted needed to be accumulated by the end of the exposure period.

Target Pests: The insect or group of insects selected for control during the fumigation. This is a required entry field for calculation of dosage.

Temperature: The degree (F or C) at the site of pest activity either in the structure, machinery, or commodity. In the case of chamber fumigations, the internal temperature of the commodities to be fumigated. This is a required entry field for calculation of dosage.

Time of Last Entry: The date and time for the last data entry (concentration) for a monitoring point.

Tool Bar: A group of functions located below the Top Menu: New, Open, Save, Print, Cut, Copy and Past.

Top Menu: The menu located at the top of the application. Menu items include File, Edit, View and Help.

Vacuum Chamber: Specially built steel chamber used for fumigation. After the commodity to be fumigated is placed in the chamber, air is evacuated by pumps. The fumigant is then introduced and rapidly fills all the air space previously occupied by air.

Volume: : Cubic (length x width x height) measurement of interior space of a Structure or Area. Measured in English units of cubic feet and metric units of cubic meters. This is a required entry field for calculation of dosage.
